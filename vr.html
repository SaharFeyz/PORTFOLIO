VR WAVE

Project Overview

DESCRIPTION

The VR Wave is a project that is done based on the concept of architectural structures based on two materials, wood and fabric. This VR device is assembled with wavy shape laser cut pieces of wood, and is aimed at users who value unconventional interesting forms as well as the functionality of objects.

MY MAJOR ROLE

  • Sketching the initial ideas and developing the final form.
  • Preparing CorelDraw files and communications with Solid Space for Lasercut parts.
  • Building the physical prototype.
  • Leading the team.
  • Researching the target audience and creating persona.

CHALLENGES

  • Creating a fully functioning, light weight, and neat VR device with an unconventional heavy material such as wood.

Prototype Process

IDEATION AND MARKER SKETCHING

Each team member (4 team members per group) had to sketch 3 ideas for the VR headset. I sketched multiple designs with markers to better visualize different ideas. Then, in a feedback session other students had to vote on a sticky note for one of the 12 ideas. For our group, my idea, the sketch on the left, received the most votes and best feedback.

Objective

  • Trying to develop a design that is airy enough to reduce the excess weight of the final product and follows an organic form that is a metaphor of architectural structures.

FIRST PHYSICAL PROTOTYPE

For the first physical prototype I used Styrene sheet and polystyrene foam known as blue/pink modeling foam.

Challenges

  • Bending the styrene sheet.
  • Sticking pink foam to styrene.

Solution

  • Using heat blower gun and a cylindrical object that can hold the shape of heated styrene.
  • Integrating the pink foam in the styrene by carefully measuring the height and width of both materials instead of trying to glue and stick them together.

Opportunities for improvement

  • Removing the boxy shape of the phone holder.

SECOND PHYSICAL PROTOTYPE

In this iteration, I removed the boxy-shape inner part of the device that holds the phone to reduce the weight of the device and to increase more consistency; instead, I added two pairs of sticks on each (inner) side of the device to keep the phone in place, and a slider (another stick) that slides underneath the phone to hold the phone and prevent it from falling down.

Challenges

  • Figuring out the best distance between eyes, lenses and phone to provide the best quality of when the 3D images of the VR app overlap.

Solution

  • Changing the curved shape of the pink foam to straight form.

Opportunities for improvement

  • Improving the ergonomics of the padding that touches the face.

FINAL PRODUCT

For the final iteration, I used laser cut plywood (with a rasterized SIAT logo) and faux leather. I iterated a few times in order to figure out the perfect size of each piece, the right distance between the lenses and phone, and more importantly the best ergonomic padding, which touches the face. Finally, because of the precision of the model, full functionality and creative design, the project received an excellent grade.

Final Reflection

ANALYSIS

The final design met the expectations, received and excellent grade, and it was light weight and airy enough to be functional and easy to hold it on the face.

TAKEAWAYS

From this project I learned that multiple iterations with different prototyping materials can significantly improve any design.
