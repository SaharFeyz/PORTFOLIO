ARC’TERYX VEILANCE

Project Overview

DESCRIPTION

In this project, our group is proposing a page extension on Arc’teryx website on the main navigation bar for Veilance that helps highlight the brand’s core value of performance innovation by enhancing the engagement phase of the customer’s online experience. This project was done in an iterative four-week process.

MY MAJOR ROLES

  • Research the client’s value proposition
  • Create Brand Model, Customer Journey Framework, and project roadmap graph
  • Research the client's business problem
  • Van UX criteria and Brand Values Analysis
  • Digital Prototype

CHALLENGES

Finding a business problem related to user experience and incorporating online intervention that leverages e-commerce strategy.

DIGITAL TOOLS USED

  • Adobe Illustrator
  • InVision
  • Figma
  • Balsamiq

Sprint Process

Target audience

  • We are targeting both existing Arc’teryx customers and new customers who seek quality brand products for urban environments

Frame

  • How can we attract more customers and create an engaging online experience without advertising the products and by just letting them speak for themselves?

Business Problem

Veilance started in the fall 2009, so it’s been 7 years. The biggest thing we have to overcome is that there are so many people that still don’t know us

— Chief Brand Director Takanori Kasuga, 2016

Insight

The goal is finding new ways to improve the activities in peoples’ lives Sometimes innovations and breakthroughs in the mainline transfer really well and work seamlessly in Veilance, but we have to be careful not to make it feel forced It has to make sense in an urban environment, and stay relevant and functional.

— Lars Mackinnon, Arc’teryx Veilance Designer, 2016

BRAND VALUES

Craftsmanship

  • Unique construction techniques to sculpt premium materials into functional products.

Performance Innovation

  • Combining high quality materials with intuitive design resulting in exceptional performance in extreme need.

Overcoming Complexities

  • A desire to reduce the effects of complex conditions to create a timeless experience.

Tangible

  • long lasting outerwear and equipment quality and material of the products

VAN UX CRITERIA

Joy

  • The subtle interactions of investigating through the style guide page, takes the audience to an outside world.

Elegance

  • Consistency of visuals and elements with simple interactions, reduce the cognitive overhead.

Clarity

  • Easy and familiar paths for a straightforward navigation, lowers the friction in the new Veilance website.

Impact

  • The audience will have an opportunity to engage more deeply in the website, that triggers them to purchase.

Final Reflection

Analysis

According to the feedback from the teaching staff, our final presentation showed a lot of improvement from the beginning of the project, and there was more clarity in the business problem and the relationship between our proposal and the business problem.

Feedback from teaching staff: My first impressions were very positive. This makes a lot more sense, the visual design in the deck and in the UI has VASTLY improved and there is a lot of care and quality evident.

Takeaways

The very valuable takeaway of this project is doing the initial research understanding the business problem, the client company, the target audience, the insights, and performing an iterative process to achieve the best results.
