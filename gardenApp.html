GARDEN CARE APP

Project Overview

DESCRIPTION

The interactive system that we focused on this project is a home-based gardening mobile app that its goal is to help novice gardeners, people whom are interested in learning how to raise one or more plants for varied personal reasons (e.g. want to have a simple house plant, want to grow vegetables for home consumption, want to be more responsible, etc.), or those who have little experience in doing so and/or do not know where to start maintaining gardening as a hobby, to achieve their goal.

MY MAJOR ROLE

  • Prototyping the app for the entire process
  • Design and illustrate all the icons
  • Sketch and illustrate the wireframes
  • Research and ideate the concept
  • Research and create the customer journey framework and competitor analysis
  • Design and prototype the micro-interaction
  • Preparing presentation slides

DIGITAL TOOLS USED

  • Principle
  • Sketch
  • Adobe Illustrator

Interactive Prototype Process

FIRST INTERACTIVE PROTOTYPE

This step required us to create a high-fidelity multilinear interactive prototype of our entire interactive system using Axure, Balsamiq, Origami, Framer, XD, HTML or other means. This prototype had to include every step and interaction that was part of the core path and core idea of the application at minimum.

Objective

We had to endeavour to complete as many other connected features as we could in order to have a broad range of interactions and user paths for testing.

I used Sketch and Principle to prototype because of its flexibility and wide range of creativity options for the core interactions and micro-interaction.

Opportunities for improvement

Improving the interface by removing the still images, replacing them with illustrated icons, and using card style shapes for browsing the categories

Challenges

  • Working with Principle for the first time, lack of experience working with the software
  • Many unwanted animation transitions appeared in the prototype
  • Simplifying the interface yet having engaging micro-interactions

Solution

I had to remove every single element in the artboard out of the group in order to prevent them from being automatically animated when a button was animated while tapping.

TESTING AND REFINING

This step required us to conduct user testing on three users. These could involve surveys and interviews, heuristic evaluations or other methods such as Think Aloud Testing, or Task Analysis. We tested our first prototype on three users. Our intention was to prompt them with a series of tasks, observe them, then ask a series of questions related to their ease of use and overall experience regarding our proposed user interface. We used a questionnaire that prompted users to perform an interaction task, and then fill out a few quick questions.

Objective

This step was crucial to understand how easily the user can finish the assigned tasks and find out the overall experience and satisfaction with the user interface.

Opportunities for improvement

In the final week the interface must decrease the confusion of user about reminder and my garden to the minimum. Also, moving forward, the bottom menu will be updated to increase readability and visibility.

Challenges

  • The interface issues we found in user testing is that there is a disconnection between the hamburger menu and bottom menu. The more important features must be in the bottom menu to facilitate the process of locating Reminder and My garden.
  • These issues created frustration and confusion for the users, and slowed down their process.

Solution

Visually help users to recognize in the first place and make important functions apparent at almost every stage.

FINAL DESIGN

Finally, we had to provide an exceptional interface design with clear affordances, consistent visual language, exceptional micro-interactions, and a completed interface that resolves a problem exceptionally well for the intended user.

User’s Goals

  1. Want to learn more about gardening
    • The essentials of gardening (planting seeds, watering, sunlight, etc.)
    • Watering: how often to water your plants, how to check if the soil is dry how to water your plants (e.g. water the soil, not the leaves)
    • The specifics of their chosen plants
  2. Attain a more mindful lifestyle through the act of gardening (patience, responsibility, learning)
    • Gain a sense of self-accomplishment
    • Create a more welcoming environment within their property with live plants (Grow vegetables for their own consumption)
    • Be able to learn on their own (e.g. be taught through an app, not a class)

App’s Features

  1. Plant Recommendations
    • Will recommend potential plants for the user to consider, based on their region of residence
  2. Categories
    • An organized database of all plant entries for easy browsing
  3. Plant Guides
    • Detailed information on a plant species, including data on growth seasons, to help in deciding which plant is right
  4. My Garden
    • A central location that displays your chosen plants, providing access to their respective Plant Guides
  5. Reminders
    • Scheduled alarms/notifications to prompt care routines
  6. Stats
    • A visual summary of the user’s gardening performance, based on the user’s app interaction

EMBEDDED ANIMATIONS

Key interactions in UI:

  • Dismissing reminders: Slide each reminder to the right of the screen; reminder continues to move off screen until it disappears. Remaining reminders in the list will move up to fill the empty space.
  • Watering: Water droplet icon on button fills up with blue to show that the plant has been watered. Over time, the “water level” drains until the droplet icon is empty. Visually communicates that the plant is “full” or “empty” of water.
  • Add Plant to My Garden: Button rotates. Whimsical animation, when paired with angled pop-up window: offers a casual, fun feel, then transforms to checkmark.
  • Pop-up Window: Slides up at an angle; dimmed background overlay slides in from left. When dismissed, the pop-up window slides back down/in direction where it came from.
  • Plant Suggestions: Plant cards are contained in a row that can be scrolled horizontally.

Key paths in UI:

  • Browsing through Categories: Pages slide up from the bottom when loaded.
  • Home page: Slides in from right side. Highlights the importance of Home as the main page with a differentiating visual introduction.
