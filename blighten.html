B-LIGHTEN LAB

Project Overview

CONCEPT SUMMARY

The idea addresses the presence of harmful ingredients in skin-lightening products and how this is still promoted by the beauty industry because lighter-skinned people are seen as better than darker-skinned people, especially with women in third-world countries. These women tend to consume a lot of skin lightening products in an attempt to change their skin color which will continue to benefit the skin-lightening industry. With some lightening creams, there are potential side effects as they may contain high levels of hydroquinone and mercury that lead to skin problems such as blisters and acne.

OUR PRODUCT

Physical — The product is a set of skin-lightening creams with variety of scents, containing hydroquinone and with different concentrations of Kojic acid (known to lighten skin as well). The concentration is derived through the Blighten-lab’s app feature (B-lighten lab + 23andMe) where it personalizes your dosage based on your genetics result. It predicts how light your skin should be based on your white-passing genetics and recommends the right product to achieve that look.

Digital — The app flow lets the user browse through current products as well as explore the 23andMe feature, get their sample in and receive their results. The recommendations based on their results can then be purchased on the app.

MY MAJOR ROLE

  • Graphic design of the physical components
  • Ideation, sketch, research
  • Developing the idea of the existing critical issue and its relation to the product during the process of the project
  • Laser cut and engraving
  • On-screen design and presentation preparation
  • Building all the physical components, including the box, packages, instruction papers, labels for the creams, ribbons for the box lids

CHALLENGES

  • Designing packages and products that are believable and provocative, yet they reflect the Critical Design strategy with subtle details that addresses the beauty standards issue
  • Designing a consistent branding for the products

SOLUTIONS

  • Thinking through personalizing the products by taking consumer's personal data such as name, skin tone information, and genetic information when they use the app. Also, partnership with 23 and me to take the genetic information seemed a decent idea to overcome the challenge of making the product provocative, controversial and believable commercial product.
  • Using the idea of gathering data and reflect that on the products, such as suggesting the dosage of bleaching chemicals for the skin, engraving the name of the consumer on the box, as well as printing the picture of the consumer’s face before and after using the bleaching creams on the package, were helpful choices to overcome the challenges.

Process

EARLY EXPLORATION

Part One — The early idea was to develop a set of make-up products that was focusing on showing the gross (e.g. animal fats)/danger (e.g. dangerous chemicals) side of what is inside the makeup that target women especially in the third world countries where make-up use is heavy. One example of this cosmetic set (EYESHADOW KIT) shows the harmful material used in cosmetics but they are covered by beautiful names that are associated with soft and good feelings.

Part Two — Continuous usage of bleaching creams are known to damage the skin especially when daily application isn’t controlled, and by having the app encourages its users in a subtle way, creating a dependence on the product even when they experience skin damage, it forces them to realize this product only contains harmful substances that will manifest in the long term.

Objective

  • The objective is to produce concise and clear conceptualizations of ideas and design statements for the project; compose and create proposals that effectively use a speculative design strategy.

SECOND EXPLORATION

Welcome to our DIY skin care home recipe. Based on the taste you have chosen, the app suggests ingredients that you can find in your home easily and mix it with the base cream that you need to order from the app. Then, you will add those ingredients at home with the base cream and have your organic natural DIY lightening cream in your hands.

Objective

In this phase the concept is almost finalized and many improvements have been made in the prototypes. The prototypes are very close to the final design in this state, but there are still some changes that have been made in the final design.

FINAL DESIGN

In the final design, we decided to eliminate the lightening cream for the baby, and focus more on the personalization of the product and how the app can entice the consumer to make the right decision for purchasing the creams. For the final product, I designed a personalized box that had the name of the consumer engraved on the lid, ribbons wrapping around the box lid with the name of the consumer printed on them, including the picture of the consumer indicating before and after using the lightening cream products, on the side of each lightening cream box in the package. The final form with more sophisticated design resulted in a more effective and successful project.

Graphical design decisions

  • Color coded packages for each dosage and the type of creams
  • Using the flower illustration of the cream scent on the boxes to enhance the visibility and faster recognition
  • Using large numbers (1, 2, 3) on the side of each set of creams to indicate the order of usage
  • Using visual hierarchical clustering of the information
  • Logo is simple, gradient and wavy conveys a soft feeling and has beauty and soft skin products connotation

TAKEAWAYS

The entire process of this project was very enjoyable, and the design approach we used worked well for the context. I learned a lot from this project, and how choosing the right design strategy, in this case critical design, can significantly change the impact of a design. Throughout the project, I was able to understand, interpret, and think about future and existing designs. Also, I learned theoretical perspectives of speculative design, methods for creating flexible and innovative designs of the future, and techniques for critiquing culture and design.
